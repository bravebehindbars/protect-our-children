          Reform

          In December of 2023, Walk A Mile In Their Shoes dedicated a report to Maddox Williams. The report was filled with recommendations on how to improve Maine's Child Welfare Systems. The areas of concern focused on for caseworkers are insufficient trainings, a dysfunctional computer system, unrealistic expectations, and unmanageable caseloads. A solution they came up with creating a team approach by onboarding case aides to complete clerical work, legal aides to asssit with any legal paperwork, and clinicians to address the medical, dental, and mental health needs of a child. The areas of concern focused on for foster parents are that their rights are disregarded (given too little resources, too little information on child, etc), and a fear of retaliation from DHHS when they share a differeing opinion. A solution recommended in this report is an organizational structure dedicated to creating a network for foster parents to be able to find support, community, and ways to develop. The areas of concern focused on for children are excessive focus on family reunification, permanency not being prioritized, and kinship placements. The recommended solution is a collection of things including prioritizing the child's safety, enforcing drug testing procedures, having the guardian ad litem having regular contact with their clients, dedicate more resources to supporting teenagers, and considering more factors relating to reunification decisions.
